<broadcast>

<broadcast> describes a broadcast used as the source of a spoken text. [8.2 Documenting the Source of Transcribed Speech 15.3.2 Declarable Elements]
Modulespoken — Transcriptions of Speech
Attributesatt.global (@xml:id, @n, @xml:lang, @xml:base, @xml:space) (att.global.rendition (@rend, @style, @rendition)) (att.global.linking (@corresp, @synch, @sameAs, @copyOf, @next, @prev, @exclude, @select)) (att.global.analytic (@ana)) (att.global.facs (@facs)) (att.global.change (@change)) (att.global.responsibility (@cert, @resp)) att.declarable (@default)

Example
<broadcast>
 <bibl>
  <author>Radio Trent</author>
  <title>Gone Tomorrow</title>
  <respStmt>
   <resp>Presenter</resp>
   <name>Tim Maby</name>
  </respStmt>
  <respStmt>
   <resp>Producer</resp>
   <name>Mary Kerr</name>
  </respStmt>
  <date when="1989-06-12T12:30:00">12 June 89, 1230 pm</date>
 </bibl>
</broadcast>
